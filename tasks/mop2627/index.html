How Does It Work?

From Academic Year 2627 onwards, Psychology students will choose modules across three levels:

  • 1 module in Year 1 (Level 4)
  • 2 modules in Year 2 (Level 5)
  • 3 modules in Year 3 (Level 6)

Your selected modules will determine your graduation pathway. Choose a focused set for a specialist pathway, or mix broadly for a General Psychology award.

Pathways

Psychology

Select a mix of modules across all pathways for a broad, flexible psychology degree. Ideal for students who want to explore many areas of psychology, keeping options open for diverse careers or further study in multiple specialisms.

Education and Child Development

Explore developmental theories, learning processes, and educational psychology. Understand how children grow, learn, and develop in diverse settings. Ideal for those interested in teaching, child welfare, or developmental support roles.

Sports and Exercise Psychology

Focus on psychological principles in sport and exercise contexts. Study motivation, performance, mental skills training, and wellbeing in athletes and active populations.

Health Psychology

Learn how psychology contributes to health, illness, and healthcare. Topics include health behaviour change, chronic illness management, stress, and healthcare communication.

Forensic Psychology

Examine psychological principles in criminal justice and legal contexts. Study offender profiling, eyewitness testimony, risk assessment, and rehabilitation.

Counselling Psychology

Explore theories and methods of psychological counselling. Develop understanding of therapeutic relationships, listening skills, and support strategies for individuals in distress.

Marketing, Business & Organisational Psychology

Apply psychology to business, marketing, and workplace settings. Topics include consumer behaviour, leadership, team dynamics, and organisational change.
